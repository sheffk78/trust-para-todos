Article I

Section 1.1 Appointment of Agent. I, {{principal_full_name}}, of {{principal_city}}, {{governing_state}}, being of sound mind, hereby appoint {{healthcare_agent_name}}, of {{healthcare_agent_address}}, {{healthcare_agent_phone}}, as my agent (my “Healthcare Agent”) to make healthcare decisions for me as authorized in this Directive.

Article II
Effective Date and Agent Authority

Section 2.1 Effective Date. My Healthcare Agent’s authority to make healthcare decisions for me shall commence when my attending physician certifies in writing that I lack the capacity to make my own healthcare decisions. My Healthcare Agent’s authority shall continue until I regain capacity, as determined by my attending physician, or until my death.

Section 2.2 Scope of Authority. My Healthcare Agent is authorized to make any and all healthcare decisions for me that I could make if I had capacity, including but not limited to:

  1. (a) To consent to, refuse, or withdraw any medical treatment, diagnostic procedure, or therapeutic intervention.
  2. (b) To select and discharge healthcare providers, hospitals, nursing homes, and other healthcare facilities.
  3. (c) To approve or disapprove diagnostic tests, surgical procedures, and programs of medication.
  4. (d) To make decisions regarding life-sustaining treatment, including the withholding or withdrawal of such treatment.
  5. (e) To make decisions regarding artificial nutrition and hydration.
  6. (f) To authorize the release of my medical records and health information as permitted by law.
  7. (g) To make decisions regarding organ and tissue donation.
  8. (h) To make decisions regarding the disposition of my remains, including funeral and burial arrangements.
  9. (i) To apply for and receive government benefits for which I may be eligible, including Medicare, Medicaid, and Social Security.
Article III
Statement of Wishes Regarding Medical Treatment

This Article expresses my wishes regarding medical treatment in specific circumstances. My Healthcare Agent shall follow these instructions to the fullest extent possible. If a situation arises that is not addressed by these instructions, my Healthcare Agent shall make decisions in accordance with my known values and beliefs, and in my best interest.

Section 3.1 General Philosophy. I believe that quality of life is as important as the prolongation of life. I direct my Healthcare Agent to consider the following values when making decisions on my behalf:

  1. (a) I wish to be kept comfortable and free from pain to the extent possible.
  2. (b) I wish to be treated with dignity and respect.
  3. (c) I wish to have my religious and spiritual beliefs respected.
  4. (d) I wish to be surrounded by my family and loved ones to the extent possible.
  5. (e) {{additional_values_statement}}

Section 3.2 Terminal Condition. If I am in a terminal condition, meaning an incurable and irreversible condition that, without the administration of life-sustaining treatment, will result in death within a relatively short time, I direct the following regarding life-sustaining treatment:

  1. (a) Life-Sustaining Treatment: I direct that life-sustaining treatment be withheld or withdrawn, and that I receive only palliative care, comfort care, and pain management.
  2. (b) Artificial Nutrition and Hydration: I direct that artificial nutrition and hydration be withheld or withdrawn if I am in a terminal condition and unable to take food and water by mouth.
  3. (c) Pain Management: I direct that I receive effective pain management and palliative care to keep me comfortable, even if such treatment may hasten my death or mask my condition.

Section 3.3 Permanent Unconsciousness. If I become permanently unconscious, meaning a condition of irreversible loss of consciousness with no reasonable expectation of regaining consciousness, I direct the following:

  1. (a) Life-Sustaining Treatment: I direct that life-sustaining treatment be withheld or withdrawn, and that I receive only palliative care, comfort care, and pain management.
  2. (b) Artificial Nutrition and Hydration: I direct that artificial nutrition and hydration be withheld or withdrawn if I am permanently unconscious and unable to take food and water by mouth.

Section 3.4 End-Stage Condition. If I am in an end-stage condition, meaning a condition caused by injury, disease, or illness that is severe and incurable, and in which I am completely dependent on others for all care, I direct the following:

  1. (a) Life-Sustaining Treatment: I direct that life-sustaining treatment be withheld or withdrawn, and that I receive only palliative care, comfort care, and pain management.
  2. (b) Artificial Nutrition and Hydration: I direct that artificial nutrition and hydration be withheld or withdrawn if I am in an end-stage condition and unable to take food and water by mouth.

Section 3.5 Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR). If my heart or breathing stops, I direct the following:

  1. (a) If I am in a terminal condition, permanently unconscious, or in an end-stage condition, I direct that CPR not be administered.
  2. (b) If I have a reasonable expectation of recovery, I direct that CPR be administered.

Section 3.6 Mechanical Ventilation. I direct that a mechanical ventilator (respirator) be used to assist my breathing only if there is a reasonable expectation of recovery. If I am in a terminal condition, permanently unconscious, or in an end-stage condition, I direct that mechanical ventilation not be used or, if already in use, be withdrawn.

Section 3.7 Dialysis. I direct that kidney dialysis be administered only if there is a reasonable expectation of recovery. If I am in a terminal condition, permanently unconscious, or in an end-stage condition, I direct that dialysis not be used or, if already in use, be withdrawn.

Article IV
Limitations on Agent’s Authority

Notwithstanding the foregoing, my Healthcare Agent shall not have the authority to:

  1. (a) Commit me to a mental health facility for more than the period permitted by applicable law, unless I am admitted voluntarily or by court order.
  2. (b) Consent to psychosurgery, sterilization, or abortion, unless such procedures are necessary to preserve my life or prevent serious and irreversible harm to my health.
  3. (c) Make decisions that are contrary to my expressed wishes as set forth in this Directive or otherwise known to my Healthcare Agent.
  4. (d) {{additional_limitation}}
Article V
Authorization to Access Medical Records

Section 5.1 HIPAA Authorization. I authorize my Healthcare Agent to receive, review, and obtain copies of my medical records and health information protected under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA) and any applicable state law. This authorization includes all protected health information, including records related to mental health treatment, substance abuse treatment, HIV/AIDS status, and genetic testing, to the fullest extent permitted by law.

Section 5.2 Duration. This authorization shall remain in effect during any period in which my Healthcare Agent has authority to make healthcare decisions for me, and shall continue after my death to the extent necessary for the administration of my estate or the disposition of my remains.

Article X
Duration and Revocation

Section 10.1 Duration. This Advance Healthcare Directive shall remain in effect until revoked by me. I understand that this Directive does not expire and shall remain in effect indefinitely unless I revoke it.

Section 10.2 Revocation. I may revoke this Advance Healthcare Directive at any time, in any manner that communicates my intent to revoke, regardless of my mental or physical condition at the time of revocation. If I revoke this Directive, I will make reasonable efforts to notify my Healthcare Agent and my healthcare providers of the revocation.

Article XI
Release and Indemnification

Section 11.2 Indemnification. My estate shall indemnify and hold harmless my Healthcare Agent against all claims, liabilities, and expenses, including reasonable attorneys’ fees, arising from my Healthcare Agent’s actions taken in good faith pursuant to this Directive.
