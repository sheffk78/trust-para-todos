Article I
Parties and Title

This Assignment of Personal Property to Trust (this “Assignment”) is made and entered into on this day of , (the “Effective Date”), by and between:

Assignor: {{grantor_name}}, an individual residing at {{settlor_1_address}} (hereinafter referred to as the “Assignor” or “Grantor”); and

Trustee: {{trustee_name}}, an individual residing at {{settlor_1_address}}, acting in the capacity of Trustee of that certain revocable living trust known as the {{trust_name}}, dated {{trust_date}} (the “Trust”) (hereinafter referred to as the “Trustee”).

The Assignor and the Trustee may be referred to herein individually as a “Party” and collectively as the “Parties.”

Article II
Description of Property Being Assigned

For valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Assignor hereby assigns, conveys, transfers, and delivers to the Trustee, in trust, all of the Assignor’s right, title, and interest in and to all of the Assignor’s personal property, wherever located, whether now owned or hereafter acquired (collectively, the “Assigned Property”). Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the Assigned Property shall include, but shall not be limited to, the following:

  1. (a) All household goods, furniture, furnishings, appliances, and effects of every kind and nature located at {{settlor_1_address}} or at any other location owned, leased, or occupied by the Assignor.
  2. (b) All jewelry, precious metals, gemstones, watches, and personal adornments.
  3. (c) All artwork, paintings, sculptures, collectibles, antiques, and decorative items.
  4. (d) All motor vehicles, watercraft, recreational vehicles, and all related titles, registrations, and accessories.
  5. (e) All digital assets, including but not limited to electronic records, electronic communications, online accounts, social media accounts, domain names, digital wallets, digital currency, software licenses, digital photographs, and any associated access credentials, codes, or keys, and all rights and interests therein.
  6. (f) All intangible personal property, including but not limited to bank accounts, brokerage accounts, securities, stocks, bonds, mutual funds, partnership interests, membership interests in limited liability companies, notes receivable, intellectual property rights, copyrights, patents, trademarks, royalties, and any other contractual or beneficial interests.
  7. (g) All insurance policies and the proceeds thereof, including but not limited to life insurance policies, annuities, and property and casualty insurance, together with all beneficial interests therein, to the extent not previously assigned to the Trust.
  8. (h) All tangible personal property of every kind and nature not specifically enumerated above, including clothing, books, tools, equipment, firearms, sporting goods, and any other personal effects.
  9. (i) All proceeds, replacements, accessions, and increments of, to, and from any of the foregoing, and all dividends, interest, distributions, income, and gains derived therefrom.

The inclusion of specific categories in this Article is for purposes of clarification and shall not be construed to limit the scope of this Assignment, which is intended to be a blanket transfer of all personal property of the Assignor, wherever located.

Article III
Declaration of Assignment

The Assignor hereby declares, covenants, and agrees that, effective as of the Effective Date, all of the Assigned Property is and shall be held by the Trustee as trust property under the terms of the Trust. The Assignor further declares that this Assignment is made voluntarily, with full understanding of its nature and effect, and without coercion, duress, or undue influence of any kind. The Assignor intends that this Assignment constitute a present transfer of the Assigned Property to the Trust, subject only to the right of revocation reserved in Article VI hereof.

Article IV
Grantor’s Representations and Warranties

The Assignor hereby represents and warrants to the Trustee as follows:

  1. (a) Ownership. The Assignor is the sole and rightful owner of the Assigned Property, holding good and marketable title thereto, free and clear of all liens, encumbrances, security interests, claims, and adverse interests of any kind, except as may be expressly disclosed in writing to the Trustee prior to the Effective Date.
  2. (b) Authority. The Assignor has full legal right, power, authority, and capacity to execute and deliver this Assignment and to transfer the Assigned Property to the Trustee as provided herein.
  3. (c) No Conflict. The execution and delivery of this Assignment, and the performance of the Assignor’s obligations hereunder, do not and shall not violate, conflict with, or constitute a breach or default under any agreement, contract, judgment, order, decree, or law applicable to the Assignor or binding upon the Assigned Property.
  4. (d) Enforceability. This Assignment constitutes the legal, valid, and binding obligation of the Assignor, enforceable against the Assignor in accordance with its terms.
  5. (e) No Prior Transfer. The Assignor has not previously assigned, transferred, pledged, or otherwise encumbered the Assigned Property in any manner inconsistent with this Assignment.
  6. (f) Full Disclosure. The Assignor has disclosed to the Trustee all material facts and circumstances known to the Assignor that relate to the Assigned Property or to the Assignor’s ability to assign the same as set forth herein.

The representations and warranties set forth in this Article IV shall survive the execution and delivery of this Assignment.

Article V
Trustee Acceptance

The Trustee, by executing this Assignment, hereby accepts the assignment and transfer of the Assigned Property and agrees to hold, manage, administer, and dispose of the Assigned Property in accordance with the terms, conditions, and purposes of the Trust. The Trustee acknowledges receipt of this Assignment and confirms that the Assigned Property shall be treated as trust property under the Trust from and after the Effective Date.

In the event that the Assignor and the Trustee are the same person, the signature of such person in both capacities shall constitute both the assignment and the acceptance required hereunder, and no further act shall be necessary to complete the transfer of the Assigned Property to the Trust.

Article VI
Reservation of Rights

The Assignor expressly reserves the right, at any time during the lifetime of the Assignor and so long as the Assignor has not been adjudicated incapacitated or incompetent, to revoke, amend, modify, or restate this Assignment, in whole or in part, and to withdraw any or all of the Assigned Property from the Trust, in accordance with the terms of the Trust and applicable law. The reservation of rights set forth in this Article VI is made in recognition of the revocable nature of the Trust and shall not be construed to impair or diminish the present transfer of the Assigned Property to the Trust, which shall remain in full force and effect until and unless expressly revoked in writing by the Assignor.

No revocation, amendment, or withdrawal pursuant to this Article VI shall be effective unless made in writing, signed by the Assignor, and delivered to the Trustee. Upon any such revocation, the Trustee shall reconvey, transfer, and deliver to the Assignor the Assigned Property, or the portion thereof so revoked or withdrawn, together with all income and appreciation attributable thereto.

Article VII
Successors and Assigns

This Assignment shall be binding upon, and shall inure to the benefit of, the Assignor and the Trustee and their respective heirs, executors, personal representatives, successors, and assigns. In the event of the death, incapacity, or resignation of the Trustee, all rights and obligations of the Trustee under this Assignment shall pass to any successor trustee appointed in accordance with the terms of the Trust.

Article VIII
Governing Law

This Assignment shall be governed by, construed, and enforced in accordance with the laws of the State of {{governing_state}}, without regard to its conflict of laws principles. The Parties agree that the courts of the State of {{governing_state}} shall have exclusive jurisdiction over any dispute arising out of or relating to this Assignment.

Article IX
Miscellaneous Provisions
  1. (a) Entire Agreement. This Assignment, together with the Trust and any schedules attached hereto, constitutes the entire agreement and understanding of the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof.
  2. (b) Amendment. This Assignment may be amended, modified, or supplemented only by a written instrument signed by the Assignor and the Trustee, except as otherwise provided in Article VI hereof.
  3. (c) Severability. If any provision of this Assignment is held to be invalid, illegal, or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.
  4. (d) Headings. The headings and captions used in this Assignment are for convenience of reference only and shall not affect the interpretation or construction of any provision hereof.
  5. (e) Counterparts. This Assignment may be executed in one or more counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original and all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument.
  6. (f) Further Assurances. The Assignor shall, upon the reasonable request of the Trustee, execute and deliver such additional documents as may be necessary or desirable to confirm, perfect, or otherwise effectuate the transfer of the Assigned Property to the Trust.
