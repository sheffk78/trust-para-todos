6. Trustee Powers (Summary)

The Trustee(s) are granted broad powers to manage, invest, and administer the Trust Estate. Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the Trustee(s) have the power to:

  1. (a) Acquire, hold, sell, exchange, convey, transfer, lease, and otherwise dispose of real and personal property, whether tangible or intangible;
  2. (b) Invest and reinvest the Trust Estate in such property and in such manner as the Trustee(s) deem prudent, without the need to diversify;
  3. (c) Borrow money and mortgage, pledge, or otherwise encumber Trust Property as security;
  4. (d) Maintain checking, savings, and other accounts in financial institutions and designate signatories therefor;
  5. (e) Open, maintain, and close brokerage, investment, and retirement accounts and execute all related instruments;
  6. (f) Employ attorneys, accountants, investment advisors, and other professional agents and pay their reasonable compensation from the Trust Estate;
  7. (g) Compromise, arbitrate, settle, or abandon claims or causes of action in favor of or against the Trust;
  8. (h) Make distributions to or for the benefit of beneficiaries as provided in the Trust Agreement; and
  9. (i) Perform all other acts and execute all other documents that the Trustee(s) deem necessary or appropriate for the proper administration of the Trust.

All powers granted to the Trustee(s) under the Trust Agreement may be exercised without court order, approval, or supervision, to the fullest extent permitted by law.
