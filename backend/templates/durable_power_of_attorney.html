Article I
Designation of Agent

Section 1.1 Appointment. I, {{principal_full_name}}, of {{principal_city}}, {{governing_state}}, hereby appoint {{agent_name}}, of {{agent_address}}, {{agent_phone}}, as my agent (my “Agent” or “Attorney-in-Fact”) to act for me in any manner permitted by law with respect to all financial and property matters.

Section 1.2 Successor Agents. If {{agent_name}} is unable, unwilling, or unavailable to serve, I appoint the following persons as successor agents, in the order listed:

  1. (a) First Successor Agent: {{first_successor_agent_name}}, of {{first_successor_agent_address}}, {{first_successor_agent_phone}}.
  2. (b) Second Successor Agent: {{second_successor_agent_name}}, of {{second_successor_agent_address}}, {{second_successor_agent_phone}}.
Article II
Effectiveness and Durability

Section 2.1 Immediate Effectiveness. This Power of Attorney is effective immediately upon execution. My Agent may act on my behalf from the date of this instrument, but I intend that my Agent shall act only if I am unable to manage my own affairs due to absence, illness, or incapacity, unless I specifically request my Agent’s assistance.

Section 2.2 Durability. This Power of Attorney shall not be affected by my subsequent incapacity or mental incompetence. This Power of Attorney shall remain in full force and effect notwithstanding my subsequent disability, incapacity, or mental incompetence, and shall continue until my death or until revoked by me.

Section 2.3 Termination. This Power of Attorney shall terminate upon the earliest of: (a) my death; (b) my revocation in writing; (c) the appointment of a guardian or conservator for my estate, in which case my Agent shall continue to act until the guardian or conservator is qualified; or (d) the expiration of any specified term.

Article III
General Grant of Authority

My Agent shall have the power to act on my behalf with respect to all financial and property matters, including but not limited to the specific powers set forth in Article IV of this instrument. My Agent may exercise each power granted herein in my name, place, and stead, in any manner that I could do if personally present and acting.

Article IV
Enumerated Powers

Without limiting the generality of Article III, my Agent shall have the following specific powers:

Section 4.1 Real Property Transactions. To purchase, sell, lease, rent, exchange, mortgage, encumber, convey, or otherwise manage any interest in real property, including the power to execute deeds, mortgages, leases, and other instruments affecting real property.

Section 4.2 Personal Property Transactions. To purchase, sell, exchange, or otherwise acquire or dispose of any tangible or intangible personal property, including household goods, vehicles, jewelry, artwork, and other personal effects.

Section 4.3 Banking and Financial Transactions. To open, maintain, or close accounts at financial institutions; to make deposits and withdrawals; to write, endorse, and cash checks; to access and empty safe deposit boxes; to execute account agreements; and to perform all other banking transactions.

Section 4.4 Securities and Investment Transactions. To buy, sell, trade, exchange, or otherwise deal in stocks, bonds, mutual funds, exchange-traded funds, and other securities; to execute brokerage agreements; to exercise stock rights and options; and to manage investment accounts.

Section 4.5 Business Operating Transactions. To operate, buy, sell, or dissolve any business owned by me; to sign contracts and agreements; to hire and fire employees; to execute all documents necessary for business operations; and to exercise all rights of a business owner.

Section 4.6 Insurance Transactions. To purchase, maintain, modify, or cancel any insurance policy; to name or change beneficiaries; to apply for and collect insurance proceeds; and to settle insurance claims.

Section 4.7 Estate, Trust, and Beneficiary Transactions. To make gifts on my behalf to the extent permitted by law; to create, amend, or revoke revocable trusts; to disclaim inheritances; to participate in trust administration; and to exercise all rights as a beneficiary of any estate or trust.

Section 4.8 Tax Matters. To prepare, sign, and file federal, state, and local tax returns; to receive and respond to tax notices; to represent me before the Internal Revenue Service and state tax authorities; to make tax elections; and to pay taxes.

Section 4.9 Claims and Litigation. To retain attorneys; to commence, prosecute, defend, or settle any claim or litigation on my behalf; to execute settlement agreements and releases; and to waive or compromise claims.

Section 4.10 Government Benefits. To apply for and receive any government benefits for which I may be eligible, including but not limited to Social Security, Medicare, Medicaid, veterans’ benefits, and disability benefits; to manage such benefits; and to represent me before government agencies.

Section 4.11 Retirement Plan Transactions. To manage, withdraw from, roll over, or make contributions to retirement accounts, including IRAs, 401(k) plans, and other qualified plans; to make beneficiary designations; to take required minimum distributions; and to exercise all rights under retirement plans.

Section 4.12 Digital Assets. To access, manage, and direct the disposition of my digital assets, including email accounts, social media accounts, online storage, digital currency and cryptocurrency, domain names, websites, and all other digital property, consistent with applicable law.

Section 4.13 General Authority. To perform all other acts and execute all other documents that my Agent deems necessary or desirable for the proper management of my affairs and property, including the power to delegate authority to others and to employ professional advisors.

Article V
Nomination of Conservator or Guardian

If a conservator or guardian of my person or estate is needed, I nominate {{agent_name}} to serve in that capacity. If {{agent_name}} is unable or unwilling to serve, I nominate {{first_successor_agent_name}} to serve in that capacity. I request that any such conservator or guardian serve without bond.

Article VI
Third-Party Reliance and Indemnification

Section 6.1 Reliance. Any third party may rely upon this Power of Attorney without inquiry into the propriety of any act by my Agent. A third party who acts in good faith reliance on this Power of Attorney shall be fully protected and shall incur no liability for any action taken in reliance thereon.

Section 6.2 Indemnification. I agree to indemnify and hold harmless any third party who relies in good faith on this Power of Attorney from any claim or liability arising from such reliance.

Section 6.3 Refusal to Honor. If any third party refuses to honor this Power of Attorney, my Agent may petition a court of competent jurisdiction to compel such third party to honor this instrument, and the third party shall be liable for all costs and expenses, including reasonable attorneys’ fees, incurred by my Agent in enforcing this Power of Attorney.

Article VII
Miscellaneous Provisions

Section 7.1 Governing Law. This Power of Attorney shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of {{governing_state}}.

Section 7.2 Severability. If any provision of this Power of Attorney is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

Section 7.3 Construction. The singular includes the plural, and the plural includes the singular. The masculine, feminine, and neuter genders include each other. “Shall” is mandatory, and “may” is permissive.

Section 7.4 Headings. Article and section headings are for convenience only and shall not affect the interpretation of this Power of Attorney.

Section 7.5 Copies. A photocopy or other electronic copy of this Power of Attorney shall have the same force and effect as the original.
